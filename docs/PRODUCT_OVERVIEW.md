# Kino Product Overview

## What Kino is for

Kino is a money management product designed to help people understand, organize, and make better decisions about their finances.

Instead of treating money as a long list of disconnected transactions, Kino gives you a clearer picture of how your money moves across wallets, bills, recurring payments, and everyday spending. It is built for people who want more control, more clarity, and a better sense of where they stand financially.

Kino can work for an individual, but it is especially useful when money is shared across a household, couple, team, or any group that needs a common view of finances.

## What people can do with Kino

### Track money in one place

Kino helps you keep your financial activity together in a single workspace. You can organize transactions, separate them by wallet, and review what came in, what went out, and what was transferred.

### Organize finances in a way that makes sense

People do not all think about money the same way. Kino lets you organize information with categories, labels, and tags so you can group spending and income in the way that feels most useful to you.

### Manage multiple wallets and shared spaces

You can work with more than one wallet and more than one workspace. This makes it easier to separate personal money, business money, shared household money, or any other setup that needs its own structure.

Kino also supports members, so more than one person can participate in managing the same financial space.

### Stay on top of recurring activity

Kino supports periodic or recurring transactions, which is useful for things like subscriptions, salary, rent, loan payments, or any other financial activity that repeats over time.

This helps reduce manual work and makes ongoing financial patterns easier to spot.

### Keep bills visible

Bills are a core part of the product. Kino helps people keep track of what is due, what has been paid, what is still pending, and how bills affect overall cash flow.

This is useful for avoiding surprises and planning ahead with more confidence.

### Save time with reusable templates and views

Kino includes templates for transactions and saved views for common ways of looking at your data.

That means less repetitive setup and faster access to the information you care about most.

### Explore trends and financial patterns

Kino goes beyond simple tracking. It includes visual dashboards and infographics that help people understand trends in income, expenses, labels, currencies, and overall financial direction.

These views are meant to help answer questions like:

- Where is most of the money going?
- How are spending habits changing over time?
- How stable or predictable is the current situation?
- What does the bigger financial picture look like?

### Generate statements and review history

Kino can produce wallet statements and make it easier to review a period of financial activity. This is helpful when someone wants a cleaner summary for record-keeping, review, or discussion with others.

### Get guidance from the finance copilot

Kino includes a finance copilot that helps people think through financial questions in a more structured way.

Rather than only showing raw numbers, it can help frame decisions, surface important factors, highlight tradeoffs, and point out possible risks or missing information.

## In simple terms

Kino is for people who want to feel less scattered about money.

It helps turn financial activity into something more understandable: what you have, what you owe, what is changing, what needs attention, and what decisions may matter next.

## Who Kino is useful for

Kino is especially helpful for:

- Individuals who want a clearer view of their finances
- Couples or families managing shared money
- People juggling multiple wallets or accounts
- Anyone who wants better visibility into bills and recurring payments
- People who prefer to make financial decisions with more context, not just raw transaction lists

## Main value of the product

The main value of Kino is clarity.

It helps people move from simply recording money activity to actually understanding it, organizing it, and using it to make better day-to-day and longer-term decisions.
